Project 1: Short Explainer Cut

Editing goal

Make the first few seconds strong enough to hold attention without overloading the viewer.

Main techniques

Jump cuts, tighter dead-space removal, caption timing, and stronger visual rhythm between scenes.

Result

A more polished short-form structure that feels faster, cleaner, and easier to follow.

Project 2: Academic Promo Edit

Editing direction

Present a study or event highlight using clear section titles, steady cuts, and balanced motion.

Visual choices

Used lower-third labels, warm overlays, scene separators, and cleaner typography rhythm.

Why it matters

This kind of structure makes educational content feel more professional and easier to watch.

Project 3: Motion Title Pack

Title timing

Motion titles need to appear quickly, stay readable, and leave before they start blocking the story.

Brand consistency

Keeping one typography system and one transition language gives the whole project a more serious identity.

Workflow value

A reusable title system saves time on future edits and improves consistency across multiple projects.

Editing Achievements

  • Improved edit clarity by separating hook, body, and closing beats more intentionally.
  • Used pacing and caption timing to make complex topics easier to follow.
  • Built a more repeatable editing process around structure instead of random changes.
