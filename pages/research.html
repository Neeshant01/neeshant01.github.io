  • Output: short scripts that explain risk plus practical prevention.

  • Supports better subject prioritization.
  • Turns large syllabi into smaller study checkpoints.

Research Methodology

  1. Start with a clear question instead of searching randomly.
  2. Collect 2 to 4 useful sources and compare the overlap between them.
  3. Write short notes in simple language so the idea can be reused later.
  4. Convert findings into output: script lines, revision notes, or action items.

Findings and Conclusions

Clear notes save time

When notes are short and structured, I can revisit a topic much faster during revision or content creation.

Comparison reduces mistakes

Looking at more than one source helps avoid weak assumptions and improves confidence in the final explanation.

Research improves communication

Good research makes it easier to explain a topic simply because the foundation is stronger.

Tips and Resources

Use a note format

Try one line each for source, key point, and how you can use it. This keeps research practical instead of endless.

Prefer source comparison

Even for simple topics, two strong references are usually better than one random article or one quick opinion.

Convert information into output

Research becomes powerful when it turns into a script, revision plan, presentation, or a clearer decision.
