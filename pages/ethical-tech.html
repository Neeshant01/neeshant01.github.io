Values / Ethical Tech

Learning technology responsibly, legally, and with a permission-first mindset.

For me, ethical tech means learning in ways that respect permission, safety, and clear public intent. The goal is awareness, improvement, and useful digital work instead of misuse.

Principles I Follow

No illegal access

I do not treat curiosity as a reason to cross legal boundaries. Permission and safety always come first.

Learn in controlled spaces

I prefer learning through documentation, own websites, local projects, and educational examples instead of risky targets.

Use skill to help people

Good technology work should improve understanding, presentation, or protection rather than create harm.

Tips and Resources

Start with cyber hygiene

Strong passwords, updates, and account security are useful ethical-tech habits before advanced topics.

Learn from trusted documentation

Official docs, awareness guides, and beginner-friendly security education are better than random risky shortcuts.

Document your intent

Clear disclaimers and visible purpose help others understand that your work is educational and responsible.
